Part 1.7.17.a.

    A set of 2 vectors can span \(\R^3\text{.}\)

  • True.

  • We know that the standard basis for \(\R^3\) contains three vectors, and as a basis, it is linearly independent. According to Theorem 1.7.1, a spanning set cannot be larger than an independent set.
