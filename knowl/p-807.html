(a) Suppose \(f : \mathbb{R}^{2} \to \mathbb{R}^{3}\) is a linear transformation such that
\begin{equation*} f \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \end{array} \right) = \left( \begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 3 \\ -4 \end{array} \right), \quad f \left( \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \end{array} \right) = \left( \begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 5 \\ 3 \end{array} \right). \end{equation*}
Compute \(\displaystyle{ f \left( \begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 7 \end{array} \right)}\text{.}\)
