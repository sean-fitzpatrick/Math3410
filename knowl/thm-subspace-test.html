Theorem 1.3.2. Subspace Test.

Let \(V\) be a vector space and let \(U\subseteq V\) be a subset. Then \(U\) is a subspace of \(V\) if and only if the following conditions are satisfied:
  1. \(\zer\in U\text{,}\) where \(\zer\) is the zero vector of \(V\text{.}\)
  2. \(U\) is closed under addition. That is, for all \(\uu_1,\uu_2\in U\text{,}\) we have \(\uu_1+\uu_2\in U\text{.}\)
  3. \(U\) is closed under scalar multiplication. That is, for all \(\uu\in U\) and \(c\in\R\text{,}\) \(c\uu\in U\text{.}\)
