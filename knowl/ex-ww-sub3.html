Exercise 3.

The trace of a square \(n \times n\) matrix \(A = (a_{ij})\) is the sum \(a_{11} + a_{22} + \cdots + a_{nn}\) of the entries on its main diagonal.
Let \(V\) be the vector space of all \(2 \times 2\) matrices with real entries.
Let \(H\) be the set of all \(2 \times 2\) matrices with real entries that have trace \(0\text{.}\) Is \(H\) a subspace of the vector space \(V\text{?}\)
(a) Does \(H\) contain the zero vector of \(V\text{?}\)
  • H contains the zero vector of V
  • H does not contain the zero vector of V
(b) Is \(H\) closed under addition?
(Hint: to show that \(H\) is not closed under addition, it is sufficient to find two trace zero matrices \(A\) and \(B\) such that \(A+B\) has nonzero trace.)
(c) Is \(H\) closed under scalar multiplication?
(Hint: to show that \(H\) is not closed under scalar multiplication, it is sufficient to find a real number \(r\) and a trace zero matrix \(A\) such that \(rA\) has nonzero trace.)
(d) Is \(H\) a subspace of the vector space \(V\text{?}\)
You should be able to justify your answer by writing a complete, coherent, and detailed proof based on your answers to parts a-c.
  • H is a subspace of V
  • H is not a subspace of V
