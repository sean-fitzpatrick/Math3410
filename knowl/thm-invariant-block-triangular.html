Theorem 5.3.4.

Let \(T:V\to V\) be a linear operator, and let \(U\) be a \(T\)-invariant subspace. Let \(B_U = \basis{u}{k}\) be a basis of \(U\text{,}\) and extend this to a basis
\begin{equation*} B = \{\uu_1,\ldots, \uu_k,\ww_1,\ldots, \ww_{n-k}\} \end{equation*}
of \(V\text{.}\) Then the matrix \(M_B(T)\) with respect to this basis has the block-triangular form
\begin{equation*} M_B(T) = \bbm M_{B_U}(T_U) \amp P\\0 \amp Q\ebm \end{equation*}
for some \((n-k)\times (n-k)\) matrix \(Q\text{.}\)
