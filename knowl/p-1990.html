In general, given an ordered basis \(B=\basis{b}{n}\) for a vector space \(V\) with standard basis \(B_0 = \basis{e}{n}\text{,}\) if we let
\begin{equation*} P = \bbm C_{B_0}(\mathbf{b}_1) \amp \cdots \amp C_{B_0}(\mathbf{b}_n)\ebm\text{,} \end{equation*}
then
\begin{align*} M_B(T) \amp = \bbm C_B(T(\mathbf{b}_1)) \amp \cdots \amp C_B(T(\mathbf{b_n})\ebm\\ \amp = P^{-1}\bbm C_{B_0}(T(\mathbf{b}_1)) \amp \cdots \amp C_{B_0}(T(\mathbf{b_n})\ebm\text{,} \end{align*}
since multiplying by \(P^{-1}\) converts vectors written in terms of \(B_0\) to vectors written in terms of \(B\text{.}\)
