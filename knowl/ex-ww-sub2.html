Exercise 2.

A square matrix \(A\) is idempotent if \(A^2 = A\text{.}\)
Let \(V\) be the vector space of all \(2 \times 2\) matrices with real entries. Let \(H\) be the set of all \(2 \times 2\) idempotent matrices with real entries. Is \(H\) a subspace of the vector space \(V\text{?}\)
  • Does \(H\) contain the zero vector of \(V\text{?}\)
    • H contains the zero vector of V
    • H does not contain the zero vector of V
  • Is \(H\) closed under addition?
    (Hint: to show that \(H\) is not closed under addition, it is sufficient to find two idempotent matrices \(A\) and \(B\) such that \((A+B)^2 \ne (A+B)\text{.}\))
  • Is \(H\) closed under scalar multiplication?
    (Hint: to show that \(H\) is not closed under scalar multiplication, it is sufficient to find a real number \(r\) and an idempotent matrix \(A\) such that \((rA)^2 \ne (rA)\text{.}\))
  • Is \(H\) a subspace of the vector space \(V\text{?}\)
    You should be able to justify your answer by writing a complete, coherent, and detailed proof based on your answers to parts 1-3.
    • H is a subspace of V
    • H is not a subspace of V
