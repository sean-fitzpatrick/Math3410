In Exercise 3.1.19, we saw that the Fourier expansion theorem gives us an efficient way of testing whether or not a vector belongs to the span of an orthogonal set. When the answer is “no”, the quantity we compute while testing turns out to be very useful: it gives the orthogonal projection of that vector onto the span of our orthogonal set. This turns out to be exactly the ingredient needed to solve certain minimum distance problems.
