Another interesting example is a car or bicycle share company. Let's say that there are 100 locations in a city where one can pick up or drop off a car/bicycle, and that a car/bike is borrowed for one hour at a time. If, for each pickup location, we know the probability that the car/bike will be returned at each location, we can represent this data in a stochastic matrix, and use the resulting Markov chain to predict the future distribution of cars/bikes.
