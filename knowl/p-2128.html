The jordan_form() command returns a pair \(P,J\text{,}\) where \(J\) is the Jordan canonical form of \(A\text{,}\) and \(P\) is an invertible matrix such that \(P^{-1}AP=J\text{.}\) You might find that the computerâ€™s answer is not quite the same as ours. This is because the Jordan canonical form is only unique up to permutation of the Jordan blocks. Changing the order of the blocks amounts to changing the order of the columns of \(P\text{,}\) which are given by a basis of (generalized eigenvectors).
