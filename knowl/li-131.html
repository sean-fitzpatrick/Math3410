Item 2.

\(\mathbf{p}\) is the closest vector in \(U\) to the vector \(\xx\text{,}\) in the sense that the distance \(d(\mathbf{p},\xx)\) is minimal among all vectors in \(U\text{.}\) That is, for all \(\uu\neq \mathbf{p}\in U\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} \len{\xx-\mathbf{p}}\lt\len{\xx-\uu}\text{.} \end{equation*}
