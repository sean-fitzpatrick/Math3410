Exercise 10.

Let \(T:P_3 \rightarrow P_3\) be the linear transformation such that
\begin{equation*} T(-2 x^2)= -4 x^2 - 4 x, \quad T(-0.5 x + 2)= 4 x^2 - 2 x + 1, \quad T(3 x^2 + 1) = -4 x - 4 . \end{equation*}
(a)
Compute \(T(1)\text{.}\)
(b)
Compute \(T(x)\text{.}\)
(c)
Compute \(T(x^2)\text{.}\)
(d)
Compute \(T(ax^2+bx+c)\text{,}\) where \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) and \(c\) are arbitrary real numbers.
